Direct & Indirect Speech

Master the art of reporting conversations. Learn how to accurately convert "spoken words" into reported sentences.

The 3 Pillars of Reported Speech

Pillar 1: Back-shifting Tenses

When we report something said in the past, the tense usually moves one step backwards in time.

• Present Simple → Past Simple
"I am tired" → He said he was tired.

• Present Continuous → Past Continuous
"I am working" → She said she was working.

• Will → Would
"I will help" → They said they would help.

Pillar 2: Changing the Perspective (Pronouns)

Pronouns must change to match the person who is doing the reporting.

• Direct: John said, "I like my new car."
• Indirect: John said that he liked his new car.

Rule: "I" becomes "He/She", "We" becomes "They", and "My" becomes "His/Her".

Pillar 3: Shifting Time and Place

Because the conversation happened at a different time, words like "now" or "here" must change.

• Now → Then
• Today → That day
• Tomorrow → The next day / Following day
• Here → There
• Yesterday → The day before

Advanced: Reporting Questions

When reporting questions, we don't use "Do/Does." We use if/whether for Yes/No questions.

• Direct: "Do you like coffee?"
• Indirect: He asked if I liked coffee.

• Direct: "Where do you live?"
• Indirect: She asked where I lived. (Notice: The word order becomes like a normal statement).
