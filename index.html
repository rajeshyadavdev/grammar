Frequently Asked Questions?

What is grammar?

Grammar is the set of rules that explains how words are formed and how they are arranged into sentences

Why is grammar important?

Grammar helps make communication clear, accurate, and easy to understand.

What is a sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.

What are the main parts of a sentence?

The main parts of a sentence are the subject and the predicate

What is a noun?

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

What is a verb?

A verb is a word that shows an action, state, or occurrence.

What is an adjective?

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun.

What is an adverb?

An adverb modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb, often telling how, when, or where.

What is a pronoun?

A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun to avoid repetition.

What is a preposition?

A preposition shows the relationship between a noun or pronoun and another word in the sentence.

What is a conjunction?

A conjunction is a word used to join words, phrases, or clauses

What is an article?

Articles are words (a, an, the) used before nouns to show definiteness.

What is the difference between “a” and “an”?

“A” is used before words that begin with a consonant sound, and “an” is used before vowel sounds.

What is tense in grammar?

Tense shows the time of an action, such as past, present, or future.

What are the main types of tenses?

The three main tenses are past, present, and future.
