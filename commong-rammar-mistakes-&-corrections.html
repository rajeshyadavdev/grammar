Common Grammar Mistakes & Corrections

Identify frequent grammar errors and learn how to fix them confidently. Turn your "Broken English" into "Professional English."

The Error Fix-It Guide

Category 1: Sentence Structure Errors

1. Run-on Sentences: Joining two complete thoughts without punctuation.
❌ "I love coffee I drink it every day."
✅ "I love coffee. I drink it every day." (Use a period or semicolon).

2. Double Negatives: Using two "no" words in one sentence.
❌ "I don't know nothing."
✅ "I don't know anything" or "I know nothing."

3. Missing "To Be": Forgetting the auxiliary verb.
❌ "They happy today."
✅ "They are happy today."

Category 2: Words That Sound the Same

1. They’re / Their / There:
• They’re (They are): "They're going home."
• Their (Belongs to them): "It is their car."
• There (Location): "Put it over there."

2. It’s vs. Its:
• It’s: Short for "It is." "It's raining."
• Its: Shows ownership. "The dog wagged its tail."

3. Your vs. You’re:
• Your: Belongs to you. "Is this your hat?"
• You’re: You are. "You're doing great!"

Category 3: Tricky Verb Agreement

1. "One of the..." Rule: The verb must be singular because the subject is "One."
❌ "One of my friends are coming."
✅ "One of my friends IS coming."

2. Uncountable Nouns: Words like Advice, Information, and News are always singular.
❌ "The news are bad."
✅ "The news IS bad."

3. "Me" vs "I":
❌ "John and me went to the park."
✅ "John and I went to the park." (Tip: Remove "John." You wouldn't say "Me went to the park.")

Quick Correction Cheat Sheet

The Mistake Why it's wrong The Correct Way
"I look forward to meet you." Phrasal verb requires -ing. "I look forward to meeting you."
"I have been in Paris in 2010." Specific past dates need Past Simple. "I was in Paris in 2010."
"She don't like it." Third person singular needs 'does'. "She doesn't like it."
"How it looks like?" "How" or "What...like", not both. "What does it look like?"
