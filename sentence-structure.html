Sentence Structure & Formation

Learn how to form clear, correct, and meaningful sentences for writing and speaking.

Mastering the Sentence

Level 1: The S-V-O Core Formula

Every clear sentence needs a "Who" and a "What."

• Subject (S): The person or thing (The boy).
• Verb (V): The action (plays).
• Object (O): The receiver (the ball).

Example: The boy plays the ball.

Level 2: Four Types of Sentences

We use different structures depending on our goal:

1. Declarative (.): To share information. "It is raining."
2. Interrogative (?): To ask a question. "Is it raining?"
3. Imperative (!): To give a command. "Stop the car!"
4. Exclamatory (!): To show strong emotion. "What a beautiful day!"

Level 3: Making Sentences Longer

Once you know the basics, use Conjunctions (and, but, or, because) to join ideas.

• Simple: "I like tea."
• Compound: "I like tea, and I like coffee."
• Complex: "I like tea because it is hot."

Warning: Fragment Errors

A sentence is a fragment if it is missing a Subject or a Verb.

❌ "Running to the store." (Who is running?)
✅ "I am running to the store."

❌ "My big brown dog." (What is the dog doing?)
✅ "My big brown dog is sleeping."
