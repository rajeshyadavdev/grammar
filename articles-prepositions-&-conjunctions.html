Articles, Prepositions & Conjunctions

Master the "glue" of the English language. Improve your accuracy by learning how to connect ideas and specify objects correctly.

The Three Connectors

Part 1: Articles (A, An, The)

Articles tell us if a noun is specific or general.

• A / An (Indefinite): Used for any one thing. "I need a pen." (Any pen).
• The (Definite): Used for a specific thing we both know. "Give me the pen." (That specific one on the table).

The Vowel Rule: Use An before a vowel sound ($a, e, i, o, u$).
An apple, An egg, An hour (starts with 'o' sound).

Part 2: Prepositions of Time & Place

These words show the relationship between a noun and the rest of the sentence.

• AT: Specific times/points. "At 5:00 PM," "At the door."
• ON: Days/Surfaces. "On Monday," "On the table."
• IN: Months/Years/Enclosed spaces. "In January," "In the room."

Pro-Tip: Think of a triangle. IN is for big things, ON is for specific things, and AT is for the most precise points.

Part 3: Conjunctions (Linking Ideas)

Conjunctions are the bridges between two thoughts.

• AND: Adds information. "I like coffee and tea."
• BUT: Shows contrast. "I like coffee, but I don't like milk."
• OR: Shows a choice. "Do you want coffee or tea?"
• BECAUSE: Shows a reason. "I drink coffee because I am tired."
• SO: Shows a result. "I was tired, so I drank coffee."

Common Confusion: For vs. Since

These are often mixed up when talking about time:

• FOR: A duration/period of time. "I have lived here for 5 years."
• SINCE: A specific starting point. "I have lived here since 2018."

Quick Reference Guide

Category Common Words Main Purpose
Articles A, An, The To identify nouns as specific or general.
Prepositions In, On, At, To, With, For To show position, time, or direction.
Conjunctions And, But, Or, So, Because To connect words, phrases, or clauses.
