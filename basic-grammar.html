How to Start: The Big Picture

Welcome! If you know nothing about grammar, think of it as building with Lego blocks. To share a thought, you just need to put three specific blocks together.

Step 1: The Sentence Formula

Every basic sentence follows the S-V-O pattern.
THE PIECES
1. Subject: Who is the story about? (The Actor)
2. Verb: What are they doing? (The Action)
3. Object: Who/What is receiving the action? (The Target)
EXAMPLE
The cat (Subject) + eats (Verb) + fish (Object).

Step 2: Your Learning Path

Start here to build a strong foundation.
PHASE 1: Building Blocks
* The Alphabet: Vowels (a, e, i, o, u) vs Consonants.
* Nouns: Naming things (Apple, Chair, London).
* Pronouns: Saying "I, You, He, She" instead of names.
PHASE 2: Making them Move
* Basic Verbs: Action words (Eat, Sleep, Go, Want).
* The Verb "To Be": Learning "I am," "You are," and "He is."

Step 3: See It In Action

Look how easily English fits together:

Who (Subject) Action (Verb) Detail (Object)

You like coffee.
He plays football.
We live in a house.

Essential Everyday Phrases

Grammar is important, but sometimes you just need to know what to say! Use these ready-made phrases to start talking to people immediately.

Part 1: Greetings & Goodbyes

How to start and end a conversation.
1. Hello! / Hi! (Friendly start)
2. Good morning / afternoon / evening. (Formal start)
3. How are you? (Asking about someone)
4. I am fine, thank you. (The standard answer)
5. Nice to meet you. (After meeting someone new)
6. Goodbye / Bye! (Leaving)
7. See you later. (Planning to meet again)
8. Have a nice day! (Wishing someone well)

Part 2: Introducing Yourself

Simple patterns to tell people who you are.
1. My name is [Name].
2. I am from [Country].
3. I live in [City].
4. I am [Age] years old.
5. I am a [Job/Student].

Part 3: Asking for Help

Use these when you don't understand something.
1. Excuse me. (To get attention)
2. Sorry? / Can you repeat that? (When you didn't hear)
3. I don't understand. (When it's too difficult)

5. How do you say [Word] in English? (Translating)
6. Please speak slowly. (If they are too fast)
7. Thank you very much! (Showing gratitude)

1. The Big Picture: Building Your First Thoughts

Grammar isn't about memorizing boring books; it is about connecting pieces to share your life with others. Let’s look at the three primary pieces you need.

Step 1: The S-V-O Formula Explained

Most English sentences follow the Subject + Verb + Object pattern.

1. The Subject (The Actor): This is the person, animal, or thing doing the action.
Example: The teacher, I, The dog, My mother.

2. The Verb (The Action): This is the movement or state of being. Every sentence MUST have a verb.
Example: eats, runs, is, likes, walks.

3. The Object (The Receiver): This is the thing getting the action.
Example: pizza, the ball, a book, English.

10 Master Examples:
1. I (S) drink (V) water (O).
2. She (S) reads (V) a story (O).
3. They (S) play (V) football (O).
4. The sun (S) shines (V). (Sometimes you don't need an object!)
5. We (S) love (V) music (O).
6. The cat (S) sleeps (V) on the bed.
7. He (S) writes (V) a letter (O).
8. You (S) speak (V) English (O).
9. Birds (S) build (V) nests (O).
10. My friend (S) buys (V) a car (O).

Step 2: Learning Path (The Roadmap)

Phase 1: The Naming Phase
Learn Nouns (things) and Pronouns (I, You, He). If you can't name it, you can't talk about it!

Phase 2: The Connecting Phase
Learn the verb "To Be" (Am, Is, Are). This allows you to say who you are and how you feel.
"I am happy." / "She is a doctor."

Phase 3: The Detail Phase
Learn Adjectives (colors, sizes) and Articles (A, An, The). This makes your sentences "pretty" and specific.
"The big red apple."

4. 10 Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

Grammar Corrections for Beginners

1. Missing Verb:
❌ "I hungry." → ✅ "I am hungry."

2. Double Subject:
❌ "My dad he is nice." → ✅ "My dad is nice."

3. Wrong Article:
❌ "I have a orange." → ✅ "I have an orange."

4. Wrong Adjective Order:
❌ "The car blue." → ✅ "The blue car."

5. The 'S' Rule:
❌ "He play tennis." → ✅ "He plays tennis."

6. Asking Questions:
❌ "You like coffee?" → ✅ "Do you like coffee?"

7. Plural Nouns:
❌ "Three book." → ✅ "Three books."

8. Negative Form:
❌ "I no want." → ✅ "I do not want."

9. Using 'I':
❌ "Me and Tom went." → ✅ "Tom and I went."

10. Always Capital 'I':
❌ "Yesterday i saw him." → ✅ "Yesterday I saw him."
