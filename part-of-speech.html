Noun Mastery

A Noun is a word that names a person, place, animals or anything. It acts as the subject or object in most sentences. Example:- Ram, Delhi, Dog, pen etc.

1- Proper Nouns

Specific names of people, places, or organizations.
DEFINITION
These name unique entities and always start with a capital letter, regardless of where they appear in a sentence.
10 Examples
1. London (Specific city)
2. Albert Einstein (Specific person)
3. Microsoft (Specific company)
4. Mount Everest (Specific mountain)
5. December (Specific month)
6. The Titanic (Specific ship)
7. Jupiter (Specific planet)
8. The Ganges (Specific river)
9. Monday (Specific day)
10. Harry Potter (Specific character)

2- Common Nouns

General names for people, places, or things.
DEFINITION
These are general categories and are not capitalized unless they start a sentence.
10 Examples
1. Teacher (General person)
2. City (General place)
3. Phone (General thing)
4. Dog (General animal)
5. Computer (General object)
6. River (General water body)
7. Book (General object)
8. Country (General place)
9. Hospital (General place)
10. Mountain (General landform)

3- Abstract Nouns

Ideas, qualities, or conditions you cannot touch.
DEFINITION
These name things you cannot see, hear, smell, taste, or touch.
10 Examples
1. Love (Emotion)
2. Honesty (Quality)
3. Freedom (Concept)
4. Bravery (Quality)
5. Success (State)
6. Knowledge (Idea)
7. Fear (Feeling)
8. Patience (Quality)
9. Justice (Concept)
10. Happiness (Emotion)

4- Concrete Nouns

Physical things perceived by the senses.
DEFINITION
Physical objects that can be touched, seen, heard, smelled, or tasted.
10 Examples
1. Apple (Taste/Sight)
2. Hammer (Touch)
3. Rain (Touch/Sight)
4. Perfume (Smell)
5. Trumpet (Sound)
6. Table (Touch)
7. Sand (Touch)
8. Ice (Touch)
9. Pizza (Taste)
10. Flowers (Smell/Sight)

5- Collective Nouns

Words representing a group as a single unit.
DEFINITION
A single word that describes a collection of people, animals, or things.
10 Examples
1. Team (Group of players)
2. Family (Group of relatives)
3. Flock (Group of birds/sheep)
4. Class (Group of students)
5. Jury (Group of judges)
6. Army (Group of soldiers)
7. Pack (Group of wolves/cards)
8. Swarm (Group of bees)
9. Audience (Group of listeners)
10. Orchestra (Group of musicians)

6- Countable Nouns

Nouns that can be counted with numbers.
DEFINITION
Nouns that have both singular and plural forms.
10 Examples
1. One car / Two cars
2. An orange / Five oranges
3. A house / Three houses
4. A student / Fifty students
5. A bottle / Ten bottles
6. A chair / Six chairs
7. An idea / Several ideas
8. A tree / Many trees
9. A pen / Four pens
10. A child / Five children

7- Uncountable (Mass) Nouns

Things that cannot be counted as individual units.
DEFINITION
They usually do not have a plural form and refer to substances or concepts.
10 Examples
1. Water (Liquid)
2. Sugar (Granular)
3. Information (Abstract)
4. Advice (Abstract)
5. Rice (Grain)
6. Milk (Liquid)
7. Gold (Material)
8. Air (Gas)
9. Furniture (Category)
10. Music (Concept)

8- Compound Nouns

Nouns formed by joining two or more words.
DEFINITION
Two words joined together to create a single noun with a new meaning.
10 Examples
1. Notebook (Note + Book)
2. Toothbrush (Tooth + Brush)
3. Mother-in-law (Hyphenated)
4. Ice cream (Spaced)
5. Swimming pool (Spaced)
6. Rainfall (Rain + Fall)
7. Sunlight (Sun + Light)
8. Blackboard (Black + Board)
9. Bedroom (Bed + Room)
10. Firefighter (Fire + Fighter)

9- Possessive Nouns

Nouns that show ownership or relationship.
DEFINITION
Usually marked by an apostrophe and the letter 's'.
10 Examples
1. The boy's ball
2. Sarah's car
3. The dog's tail
4. The teachers' lounge (Plural)
5. India's capital
6. The bird's nest
7. The doctor's office
8. My father's watch
9. The company's logo
10. The ocean's waves

Verb Mastery

A Verb is a word that expresses an action, an occurrence, or a state of being. It is the engine of the sentence.

1- Action Verbs (Physical)

Words that describe physical movements.
DEFINITION
These verbs describe actions that can be seen or heard being performed by a subject.
10 Examples
1. Run (The boy runs fast)
2. Eat (We eat lunch together)
3. Write (She writes in her diary)
4. Jump (The cat jumps on the fence)
5. Swim (They swim in the pool)
6. Speak (He speaks loudly)
7. Dance (We dance at the party)
8. Build (They build a new house)
9. Clean (I clean my room)
10. Play (Children play in the park)

2- Action Verbs (Mental)

Actions that happen inside the mind.
DEFINITION
These describe intellectual processes or feelings that aren't necessarily visible physical movements.
10 Examples
1. Think (I think it will rain)
2. Believe (She believes in ghosts)
3. Guess (Can you guess the answer?)
4. Remember (I remember your name)
5. Forget (Don't forget your keys)
6. Wonder (I wonder where they are)
7. Know (We know the truth)
8. Hope (I hope you win)
9. Understand (Do you understand?)
10. Imagine (Imagine a world of peace)

3- Linking Verbs

Verbs that connect the subject to more information.
DEFINITION
They do not show action. Instead, they link the subject to an adjective or noun that describes it.
10 Examples
1. Is (He is a doctor)
2. Am (I am tired)
3. Are (They are happy)
4. Was (It was cold yesterday)
5. Were (We were late)
6. Seem (You seem upset)
7. Become (He became a leader)
8. Feel (This cloth feels soft)
9. Appear (She appears confident)
10. Smell (The flowers smell sweet)

4- Auxiliary (Helping) Verbs

Verbs used together with a main verb.
DEFINITION
They help express the tense, mood, or voice of the main verb.
10 Examples
1. Have (I have finished)
2. Has (She has gone)
3. Do (I do not like tea)
4. Does (He does not care)
5. Did (They did not arrive)
6. Is (She is singing)
7. Are (They are playing)
8. Was (I was sleeping)
9. Were (We were talking)
10. Been (They have been waiting)

5- Modal Verbs

Verbs that show possibility, ability, or necessity.
DEFINITION
A special type of helping verb that never changes its form.
10 Examples
1. Can (I can swim)
2. Could (It could happen)
3. Will (I will help you)
4. Would (Would you like tea?)
5. Shall (We shall see)
6. Should (You should rest)
7. May (May I come in?)
8. Might (He might be late)
9. Must (You must go now)
10. Ought to (We ought to help)

6- Transitive Verbs

Verbs that require a direct object.
DEFINITION
The action of the verb is "transferred" to a person or thing (the object).
10 Examples
1. Buy (I bought a car)
2. Eat (She ate an apple)
3. Love (I love music)
4. Kick (He kicked the ball)
5. Read (She reads a book)
6. Send (Send the email)
7. Catch (Catch the bus)
8. Give (Give me the pen)
9. Cut (Cut the paper)
10. Find (I found my keys)

7- Intransitive Verbs

Verbs that do not need a direct object.
DEFINITION
The action is complete on its own without needing to act upon something else.
10 Examples
1. Sleep (The baby sleeps)
2. Arrive (The train arrived)
3. Laugh (Everyone laughed)
4. Cry (The child cried)
5. Fall (Leaves fall)
6. Sit (Please sit)
7. Stand (They stood up)
8. Exist (Disputes exist)
9. Die (The flowers died)
10. Wait (We waited for hours)

8- Phrasal Verbs

Verbs combined with a preposition or adverb.
DEFINITION
A phrase that has a different meaning than the original verb alone.
10 Examples
1. Give up (Stop trying)
2. Look for (Search)
3. Pick up (Lift/Collect)
4. Get out (Leave)
5. Run away (Escape)
6. Take off (Remove/Start flight)
7. Turn on (Start a device)
8. Wake up (Stop sleeping)
9. Call off (Cancel)
10. Break down (Stop working)

9- Stative Verbs

Verbs describing states, not actions.
DEFINITION
These are usually not used in continuous (-ing) tenses.
10 Examples
1. Like (I like pizza)
2. Hate (I hate cold weather)
3. Love (I love my family)
4. Prefer (I prefer tea)
5. Own (She owns a house)
6. Belong (It belongs to me)
7. Believe (I believe you)
8. Cost (It costs ten dollars)
9. Need (I need help)
10. Want (I want water)

Adjective Mastery

An Adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or a pronoun. It adds detail, color, and precision to your sentences.

1- Descriptive Adjectives

Words that describe the quality or state of a noun.
DEFINITION
These are the most common adjectives that tell us about the size, shape, color, or trait of something.
10 Examples
1. Beautiful (A beautiful garden)
2. Large (A large elephant)
3. Blue (The blue ocean)
4. Round (A round table)
5. Brave (A brave soldier)
6. Hot (Hot coffee)
7. Old (An old book)
8. Sweet (Sweet candy)
9. Silent (A silent room)
10. Sharp (A sharp knife)

2- Quantitative Adjectives

Words that describe the quantity of a noun.
DEFINITION
These answer the question "how much?" or "how many?"
10 Examples
1. Some (I need some water)
2. Much (There isn't much time)
3. Little (A little sugar)
4. Enough (I have enough food)
5. All (All students passed)
6. Half (Half an apple)
7. Many (Many people came)
8. Whole (The whole cake)
9. Any (Is there any milk?)
10. Sufficient (Sufficient evidence)

3- Proper Adjectives

Adjectives derived from proper nouns.
DEFINITION
These usually describe origins, languages, or specific cultures and are always capitalized.
10 Examples
1. Indian (Indian food)
2. Italian (Italian leather)
3. Shakespearean (Shakespearean plays)
4. Christian (Christian traditions)
5. Victorian (Victorian architecture)
6. Chinese (Chinese tea)
7. African (African safari)
8. Parisian (Parisian fashion)
9. American (American dream)
10. Japanese (Japanese electronics)

4- Demonstrative Adjectives

Words used to point out specific people or things.
DEFINITION
They indicate the position of a noun in space or time.
10 Examples
1. This (This pen is mine)
2. That (That house is old)
3. These (These shoes are new)
4. Those (Those birds are flying)
5. Such (I hate such behavior)
6. Other (The other side)
7. Another (Give me another chance)
8. Former (The former president)
9. Latter (The latter option)
10. Same (The same color)

5- Possessive Adjectives

Words that show ownership or possession.
DEFINITION
They are always followed by a noun to show who owns that noun.
10 Examples
1. My (My car is outside)
2. Your (Is this your book?)
3. His (His father is a doctor)
4. Her (Her dress is red)
5. Its (The cat licked its paw)
6. Our (Our house is small)
7. Their (Their team won)
8. Whose (Whose phone is this?)
9. My (My favorite color is blue)
10. Your (Your breakfast is ready)

6- Interrogative Adjectives

Words used with nouns to ask questions.
DEFINITION
These words act as adjectives when they are followed immediately by a noun.
10 Examples
1. Which (Which path should we take?)
2. What (What time is it?)
3. Whose (Whose bag is this?)
4. Which (Which color do you prefer?)
5. What (What language do you speak?)
6. Whose (Whose keys are on the floor?)
7. Which (Which car is yours?)
8. What (What subject is difficult?)
9. Whose (Whose turn is it?)
10. Which (Which movie did you watch?)

7- Comparative Adjectives

Used to compare differences between two objects.
RULES
Add '-er' for short words or 'more' for long words.
10 Examples
1. Faster (A plane is faster than a car)
2. Stronger (He is stronger than me)
3. More expensive (Gold is more expensive than silver)
4. Better (This is better than that)
5. Smaller (A mouse is smaller than a cat)
6. Higher (The bird flew higher than the tree)
7. More beautiful (This flower is more beautiful)
8. Worser (Incorrect, use 'Worse')
9. Taller (Sarah is taller than John)
10. Harder (Diamond is harder than glass)

8- Superlative Adjectives

Used to describe the upper or lower limit of a quality.
RULES
Add '-est' for short words or 'most' for long words.
10 Examples
1. Fastest (The fastest runner)
2. Strongest (The strongest man)
3. Most expensive (The most expensive watch)
4. Best (The best day of my life)
5. Smallest (The smallest kitten)
6. Highest (The highest mountain)
7. Most beautiful (The most beautiful view)
8. Worst (The worst movie ever)
9. Tallest (The tallest building)
10. Hardest (The hardest exam)

9- Indefinite Adjectives

Used to describe a noun in a non-specific way.
DEFINITION
These adjectives point to non-specific people or things.
10 Examples
1. Several (Several people left)
2. Few (Few students attended)
3. Many (Many dreams come true)
4. Each (Each child got a toy)
5. Every (Every seat was taken)
6. Either (Either way is fine)
7. Neither (Neither team scored)
8. Both (Both hands are dirty)
9. Most (Most kids like chocolate)
10. Some (Some birds cannot fly)

Adverb Mastery

An Adverb is a word that modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. It provides details about how, when, where, and to what extent an action happens.

1- Adverbs of Manner

Tell us how an action is performed.
DEFINITION
These adverbs usually follow the main verb or the object and often end in '-ly'.
10 Examples
1. Quickly (He ran quickly)
2. Slowly (She walked slowly)
3. Happily (They played happily)
4. Loudly (The dog barked loudly)
5. Quietly (Please speak quietly)
6. Carefully (Handle it carefully)
7. Easily (He passed easily)
8. Well (She sings well)
9. Fast (He drives fast)
10. Bravely (They fought bravely)

2- Adverbs of Time

Tell us when an action happens.
DEFINITION
These adverbs describe the timing or duration of an event.
10 Examples
1. Today (I will do it today)
2. Yesterday (We met yesterday)
3. Tomorrow (See you tomorrow)
4. Now (I am busy now)
5. Then (He was young then)
6. Soon (Dinner will be ready soon)
7. Later (I'll call you later)
8. Already (I have already eaten)
9. Recently (I saw her recently)
10. Tonight (Let's go out tonight)

3- Adverbs of Place

Tell us where an action happens.
DEFINITION
These adverbs describe the location, direction, or distance of an action.
10 Examples
1. Here (Come here)
2. There (Put it there)
3. Everywhere (I searched everywhere)
4. Nowhere (It was nowhere to be found)
5. Upstairs (She is upstairs)
6. Downstairs (Go downstairs)
7. Nearby (A park is nearby)
8. Far (He lives far away)
9. Inside (Come inside)
10. Outside (It is raining outside)

4- Adverbs of Frequency

Tell us how often an action happens.
DEFINITION
These indicate the regularity or repetition of an event.
10 Examples
1. Always (I always wake up early)
2. Often (We often go swimming)
3. Usually (He usually walks to work)
4. Sometimes (I sometimes eat pizza)
5. Rarely (It rarely rains here)
6. Never (I never lie)
7. Seldom (He is seldom late)
8. Frequently (She visits frequently)
9. Occasionally (We meet occasionally)
10. Daily (I exercise daily)

5- Adverbs of Degree

Tell us the intensity or extent of something.
DEFINITION
These answer the question "how much?" or "to what extent?".
10 Examples
1. Very (It is very hot)
2. Too (The tea is too sweet)
3. Extremely (She is extremely smart)
4. Quite (The movie was quite good)
5. Almost (I am almost done)
6. Enough (He is tall enough)
7. Completely (I completely agree)
8. Barely (I can barely see)
9. Rather (It is rather cold)
10. Totally (I totally forgot)

6- Adverbs of Certainty

Express how sure we are about an action.
DEFINITION
These indicate the level of confidence in the statement being made.
10 Examples
1. Certainly (I will certainly come)
2. Definitely (You are definitely right)
3. Probably (He will probably be late)
4. Surely (Surely you are joking)
5. Maybe (Maybe it will rain)
6. Perhaps (Perhaps she is busy)
7. Clearly (He is clearly the winner)
8. Obviously (Obviously, it's a mistake)
9. Doubtless (It is doubtless true)
10. Possibly (I will possibly go)

7- Conjunctive Adverbs

Used to connect two independent clauses.
DEFINITION
These provide a transition between ideas within a sentence or between sentences.
10 Examples
1. However (I like tea; however, I prefer coffee)
2. Therefore (It rained; therefore, the match was cancelled)
3. Moreover (It's cheap; moreover, it's healthy)
4. Consequently (He slept late; consequently, he missed the bus)
5. Otherwise (Hurry; otherwise, you'll be late)
6. Furthermore (The house is big; furthermore, it has a pool)
7. Meanwhile (He cooked; meanwhile, she set the table)
8. Instead (They didn't go out; instead, they watched TV)
9. Likewise (I love hiking; likewise, I love camping)
10. Nevertheless (It was cold; nevertheless, they went swimming)

8- Interrogative Adverbs

Used to ask questions about an action.
DEFINITION
These are placed at the beginning of a question.
10 Examples
1. Why (Why are you late?)
2. Where (Where do you live?)
3. When (When is your birthday?)
4. How (How do you make this?)
5. Where (Where did I put my keys?)
6. How (How often do you exercise?)
7. Why (Why did he leave?)
8. When (When will the rain stop?)
9. How (How much does it cost?)
10. Where (Where are we going?)

9- Relative Adverbs

Used to introduce a relative clause.
DEFINITION
These link a noun or pronoun to a clause that provides more information.
10 Examples
1. Where (The house where I was born)
2. When (The time when we first met)
3. Why (The reason why I am here)
4. Where (The city where they live)
5. When (The day when the war ended)
6. Why (The purpose why it was built)
7. Where (The room where he works)
8. When (The month when it snows)
9. Why (The excuse why he was absent)
10. Where (The place where the treasure is hidden)

Pronoun Mastery

A Pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun. Pronouns prevent the need for repetitive naming and make sentences flow more naturally.

1- Personal Pronouns

Words used to represent specific people or things.
DEFINITION
These pronouns change form based on whether they are the subject or the object of the sentence.
10 Examples
1. I (I am a teacher)
2. You (You are my friend)
3. He (He likes music)
4. She (She is working)
5. It (It is a sunny day)
6. We (We went to the park)
7. They (They are playing)
8. Me (Give the pen to me)
9. Him (I saw him yesterday)
10. Us (They invited us)

2- Possessive Pronouns

Words that show ownership or possession.
DEFINITION
Unlike possessive adjectives, these pronouns stand alone and do not need a noun following them.
10 Examples
1. Mine (This book is mine)
2. Yours (Is this yours?)
3. His (The blue car is his)
4. Hers (The choice was hers)
5. Ours (The house is ours)
6. Theirs (The victory is theirs)
7. Mine (That idea of mine)
8. Yours (The fault is yours)
9. His (That responsibility is his)
10. Hers (The responsibility is hers)

3- Reflexive Pronouns

Used when the subject and object are the same.
DEFINITION
These pronouns end in '-self' or '-selves' and refer back to the subject of the sentence.
10 Examples
1. Myself (I hurt myself)
2. Yourself (Do it yourself)
3. Himself (He washed himself)
4. Herself (She saw herself in the mirror)
5. Itself (The cat cleaned itself)
6. Ourselves (We helped ourselves)
7. Yourselves (Please behave yourselves)
8. Themselves (They enjoyed themselves)
9. Myself (I told myself to stay calm)
10. Itself (The door closed by itself)

4- Relative Pronouns

Used to connect a clause or phrase to a noun.
DEFINITION
These pronouns introduce relative clauses that provide more information about the noun.
10 Examples
1. Who (The man who called)
2. Whom (The person whom I met)
3. Whose (The boy whose dog ran away)
4. Which (The car which I bought)
5. That (The house that is for sale)
6. Who (The teacher who helped me)
7. Which (The movie which we watched)
8. That (The book that I read)
9. Whose (A woman whose daughter is a doctor)
10. Whom (To whom it may concern)

5- Demonstrative Pronouns

Words used to point out specific things.
DEFINITION
They indicate the location or number of items being referred to.
10 Examples
1. This (This is my house)
2. That (That is a great idea)
3. These (These are my friends)
4. Those (Those are beautiful flowers)
5. This (What is this?)
6. That (I like that)
7. These (Check these out)
8. Those (Look at those)
9. This (This is the end)
10. That (That was a mistake)

6- Interrogative Pronouns

Used to ask questions about an unknown noun.
DEFINITION
These pronouns are used at the beginning of a sentence to form a question.
10 Examples
1. Who (Who is at the door?)
2. What (What is your name?)
3. Which (Which is your car?)
4. Whose (Whose is this bag?)
5. Whom (Whom did you see?)
6. What (What do you want?)
7. Who (Who won the game?)
8. Which (Which do you prefer?)
9. What (What happened?)
10. Who (Who told you that?)

7- Indefinite Pronouns

Used to refer to people or things in a general way.
DEFINITION
These pronouns do not refer to any specific person, amount, or thing.
10 Examples
1. Anyone (Can anyone hear me?)
2. Someone (Someone is knocking)
3. Everything (Everything is ready)
4. Both (Both are welcome)
5. Many (Many were invited)
6. Few (Few turned up)
7. Each (Each had a task)
8. All (All is lost)
9. Nothing (Nothing is impossible)
10. Some (Some like it hot)

8- Reciprocal Pronouns

Used when two or more people carry out an action.
DEFINITION
These indicate that an action is mutual or returned.
10 Examples
1. Each other (They love each other)
2. One another (Help one another)
3. Each other (They looked at each other)
4. One another (We should trust one another)
5. Each other (The twins hate each other)
6. One another (Students greeted one another)
7. Each other (Give each other gifts)
8. One another (They fought one another)
9. Each other (We call each other daily)
10. One another (Be kind to one another)

Preposition Mastery

A Preposition is a word used to link nouns, pronouns, or phrases to other words within a sentence. They act as "direction signs" for time, place, and logic.

1- Prepositions of Time

Used to indicate when something happens.
DEFINITION
These help clarify specific times, days, months, or years.
10 Examples
1. At (At 5:00 PM)
2. On (On Monday)
3. In (In the morning)
4. During (During the holidays)
5. Since (Since 1995)
6. For (For two hours)
7. Before (Before sunset)
8. After (After the meeting)
9. Until (Until tomorrow)
10. By (By next week)

2- Prepositions of Place

Used to show the position or location of something.
DEFINITION
These answer the question "Where?" regarding the noun or pronoun.
10 Examples
1. On (On the table)
2. In (In the box)
3. At (At the door)
4. Under (Under the bed)
5. Over (Over the bridge)
6. Between (Between two chairs)
7. Behind (Behind the curtain)
8. Beside (Beside the pool)
9. Among (Among the flowers)
10. Near (Near the station)

3- Prepositions of Direction

Used to show movement from one place to another.
DEFINITION
These describe the path or destination of a movement.
10 Examples
1. To (Go to school)
2. Into (Jump into the water)
3. Onto (Step onto the stage)
4. Through (Walk through the tunnel)
5. Across (Swim across the river)
6. Towards (Run towards the goal)
7. From (A letter from home)
8. Off (Get off the bus)
9. Up (Go up the stairs)
10. Down (Walk down the street)

4- Prepositions of Agent

Used to show the relationship between a noun and an action.
DEFINITION
These indicate the "doer" or the instrument used for an action.
10 Examples
1. By (A book by Dickens)
2. With (Written with a pen)
3. By (Made by hand)
4. With (Cut with a knife)
5. By (Destroyed by fire)
6. With (Mixed with a spoon)
7. By (Sent by post)
8. With (Opened with a key)
9. By (Painted by Picasso)
10. With (Painted with a brush)

5- Phrasal Prepositions

A group of words that function as a single preposition.
DEFINITION
These consist of two or more words that act together to link nouns.
10 Examples
1. In front of (In front of the house)
2. Instead of (Instead of tea)
3. According to (According to the news)
4. Due to (Due to rain)
5. Next to (Next to the car)
6. Because of (Because of the noise)
7. Along with (Along with my friends)
8. On top of (On top of the world)
9. In spite of (In spite of the cold)
10. Out of (Out of luck)

Conjunction Mastery

A Conjunction is a word used to connect words, phrases, or clauses. They act as the "glue" of the English language, allowing us to build complex and elegant sentences.

1- Coordinating Conjunctions

Used to connect words or phrases of equal importance.
RULES
Remember the acronym FANBOYS (For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So).
10 Examples
1. And (I like tea and coffee)
2. But (He is small but strong)
3. Or (Do you want tea or juice?)
4. So (It was raining, so I stayed home)
5. Yet (He studied hard, yet he failed)
6. For (I drank water, for I was thirsty)
7. Nor (He doesn't like cats, nor does he like dogs)
8. And (She sang and danced)
9. But (I wanted to go, but I was tired)
10. Or (Eat now or later)

2- Subordinating Conjunctions

Used to join a dependent clause to an independent clause.
RULES
These establish a relationship of time, cause and effect, or condition.
10 Examples
1. Because (I stayed home because it rained)
2. Although (Although it was cold, he went out)
3. If (If it rains, we will cancel)
4. Since (I haven't seen him since Monday)
5. While (I listened while she spoke)
6. Unless (You won't pass unless you study)
7. Until (Wait here until I return)
8. After (We left after the movie ended)
9. Before (Wash your hands before eating)
10. Though (He is happy, though he is poor)

3- Correlative Conjunctions

Pairs of conjunctions that work together.
RULES
These must always be used in pairs to connect balanced sentence parts.
10 Examples
1. Either... or (Either stay or leave)
2. Neither... nor (Neither Tom nor Jerry came)
3. Both... and (Both Alice and Bob are here)
4. Not only... but also (Not only smart but also kind)
5. Whether... or (Whether you like it or not)
6. Scarcely... when (Scarcely had I begun when it rained)
7. Rather... than (I would rather walk than drive)
8. Such... that (It was such a cold day that I stayed in)
9. No sooner... than (No sooner had he left than it started raining)
10. As... as (He is as tall as his father)

Interjection Mastery

An Interjection is a word or phrase used to express strong feeling or sudden emotion. They usually stand alone and are often followed by an exclamation mark.

1- Joy and Greeting

Used to express happiness or to welcome someone.
DEFINITION
These words set a positive or friendly tone for the conversation.
10 Examples
1. Hurrah! (Hurrah! We won the match!)
2. Wow! (Wow! That view is amazing!)
3. Yay! (Yay! It's finally Friday!)
4. Hello! (Hello! How are you?)
5. Hi! (Hi! Long time no see!)
6. Hey! (Hey! Look over here!)
7. Aha! (Aha! I found the secret!)
8. Yippee! (Yippee! School is over!)
9. Cheers! (Cheers! To your success!)
10. Welcome! (Welcome! Please come in!)

2- Sorrow and Pain

Used to express sadness, regret, or physical hurt.
DEFINITION
These convey negative emotions or sudden physical reactions to pain.
10 Examples
1. Alas! (Alas! He is no more!)
2. Ouch! (Ouch! That needle hurt!)
3. Oh! (Oh! I am so sorry for your loss!)
4. Ah! (Ah! I've lost my keys again!)
5. Ow! (Ow! My tooth is aching!)
6. Oops! (Oops! I spilled the milk!)
7. Uh-oh! (Uh-oh! I think I'm in trouble!)
8. Phew! (Phew! That was a close call!)
9. Dear me! (Dear me! What a tragedy!)
10. Good grief! (Good grief! This is terrible!)

3- Surprise and Attention

Used to grab someone's focus or show shock.
DEFINITION
These words indicate a sudden realization or a need for silence/focus.
10 Examples
1. What! (What! You're moving to Japan?)
2. Hush! (Hush! The baby is sleeping!)
3. Listen! (Listen! Do you hear that?)
4. Behold! (Behold! The king arrives!)
5. Shh! (Shh! Keep quiet in the library!)
6. My God! (My God! That was incredible!)
7. Look! (Look! There's a rainbow!)
8. Oh my! (Oh my! How you've grown!)
9. Eek! (Eek! There is a spider!)
10. Hark! (Hark! The bells are ringing!)
